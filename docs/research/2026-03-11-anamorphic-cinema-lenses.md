### Finding 2: ARRI Master Anamorphic Flare Sets

- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Brand**: ARRI / Zeiss
- **Series**: Master Anamorphic Flare
- **Squeeze Factor**: 2x
- **Mount**: PL (LDS)
- **Sensor Coverage**: Super 35 (same as standard MA)

**NOT a separate lens series.** The Flare Sets are replaceable front and rear glass elements with special coatings that can be swapped into the standard Master Anamorphic lenses using the ARRI Master Anamorphic Toolkit. Available for all 9 focal lengths (28, 35, 40, 50, 60, 75, 100, 135, 180mm). Each set provides 3 additional looks: front element only, rear element only, or both combined. The coatings encourage flaring, ghosting, and veiling glare while preserving resolution and optical performance.

- **Supporting sources**:
  - [ARRI Press Release - New Master Anamorphic Flare Sets](https://www.arri.com/en/company/press/press-releases-2015/new-master-anamorphic-flare-sets-announced)
  - [B&H Photo - ARRI MA Flare Set MA50](https://www.bhphotovideo.com/c/product/1287736-REG/arri_k2_0006799_50mm_master_anamorphic_flare.html)

## Contradictions

- **Great Joy 50mm squeeze factor inconsistency**: Early models had variable squeeze (1.68x-1.8x) depending on focus distance. Later production corrected to constant 1.8x. Sources agree on this being an initial manufacturing issue.
  - [Newsshooter - Great Joy 50mm](https://www.newsshooter.com/2022/05/11/great-joy-50mm-t2-9-1-8x-anamorphic-lens-review/) - Noted variability
  - [Newsshooter - Great Joy 85mm](https://www.newsshooter.com/2022/12/11/great-joy-85mm-t2-9-1-8x-anamorphic-lens-review/) - Confirmed constant 1.8x

- **STATERA focal lengths**: Some sources list 95mm as part of the set, others list 100mm. The OFG page lists both 95mm and 100mm in different contexts. The CineD article lists 35, 40, 50, 75, 100mm with T2.1 and 135mm at T2.9 (6-lens set at $165K). The OFG page also mentions a 95mm.
  - [CineD](https://www.cined.com/statera-full-frame-anamorphic-primes-by-old-fast-glass-and-ancient-optics-introduced/) - Lists 35, 40, 50, 75, 100, 135mm
  - [Old Fast Glass](https://www.oldfastglass.com/statera) - Mentions 95mm in some contexts

- **Xelmus Apollo 100mm T-stop**: OFG lists MKI at T2.5, MKII/MKIII at T2.3. Newsshooter/CineD list T2.8. The discrepancy is likely due to MKI vs later revisions.
  - [Old Fast Glass](https://www.oldfastglass.com/xelmus-anamorphic) - T2.5 (MKI) / T2.3 (MKII/III)
  - [CineD](https://www.cined.com/xelmus-releases-3-new-apollo-full-frame-anamorphic-primes/) - T2.8

- **Panavision Ultra Speed-Z**: Not anamorphic. Listed in the request but confirmed as spherical lenses.
  - [Panavision Official](https://www.panavision.com/camera-and-optics/optics/product-detail/us-ultra-speed) - Spherical primes
