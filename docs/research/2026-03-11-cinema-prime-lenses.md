## Contradictions

### Canon CN-E vs Sumire vs CN-R Optical Design
- Some sources describe the Sumire as having identical optics to the CN-E with different coatings, while others describe them as having distinct optical designs optimized for different rendering.
  - [Canon Newsroom](https://www.usa.canon.com/newsroom/2019/20190403-sumire) - Describes unique optical design for Sumire
  - [Canon Asia](https://snapshot.canon-asia.com/article/eng/sumire-vs-cn-r-cinema-prime-lenses-whats-the-difference) - Compares CN-R and Sumire differences

### Cooke S7/i -- "Full Frame Plus" vs "Full Frame" naming
- The official Cooke website now just calls them "S7/i FF" while some dealers and older press still use "S7/i Full Frame Plus". The expanded focal lengths (16, 21, 27, 60 Macro, 90 Macro, 150 Macro) are part of the same series, not a separate "Plus" line.
  - [Cooke Official](https://cookeoptics.com/lens/cooke-s7-i-ff/) - Uses "S7/i"
  - [AbelCine](https://www.abelcine.com/buy/lenses-accessories/cine-lenses/cooke-s7-i-full-frame-plus-primes) - Uses "Full Frame Plus"

### Schneider Xenon FF-Prime Mark II
- No evidence found of a "Mark II" version. The only variant is the Cine-Tilt version.

### Zeiss Supreme Prime 150mm T-stop
- One source lists 150mm as T1.8, consistent with official Zeiss data. Earlier sources sometimes listed it as T1.5 before the final production specs were confirmed.
