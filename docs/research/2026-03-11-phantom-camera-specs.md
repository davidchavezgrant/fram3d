## Findings

### Finding 1: Phantom Flex4K Sensor and Performance
- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Supporting sources**:
  - [VFX Camera Database - Phantom Flex4K](https://vfxcamdb.com/vision-research-phantom-flex4k/) - Sensor: 27.6 mm x 15.5 mm at 4096x2304 (16:9). CMOS with rolling shutter. 6.75 um pixel pitch.
  - [No Film School - Phantom VEO4K-PL](https://nofilmschool.com/2017/09/phantom-veo4k-pl) - Confirms 27.6 mm x 15.5 mm sensor, 6.75 um pixel pitch. Same sensor as VEO4K-PL.
  - [Phantom Flex4K Datasheet search results](https://www.phantomhighspeed.com/-/media/project/ameteksxa/visionresearch/documents/datasheets/web/wdsflex4k.pdf) - 938 fps at 4096x2304, 1000 fps at 4096x2160, 1984 fps at 2048x1080, 2949 fps at 1280x720. Super-35 mm sensor with 31.7 mm diagonal.
  - [AbelCine - Phantom Flex4K](https://www.abelcine.com/buy/cameras-accessories/digital-cinema-cameras/phantom-flex4k-128gb-high-speed-color-camera-pl-mount) - PL mount standard, also Canon EF and Nikon F available.
- **Year released**: 2013 (announced NAB 2013, shipped October 2013)
- **Lens mount**: Interchangeable -- PL, Canon EF (electronic), Nikon F
- **Sensor format**: Super 35
- **Notes**: The Flex4K and VEO4K-PL share the same 9.4 Mpx sensor (4096x2304, 6.75 um pixels).

Standard mode: 1,459 fps at full res. 2,571 fps at 1920x1080. 5,600 fps at 1280x720.

## Contradictions

### TMX 7510 Sensor Height: 14.8 mm vs 15.4 mm
- **Source A**: [Original March 2021 datasheet](https://www.phantomhs.ru/upload/iblock/9b1/%D0%91%D1%80%D0%BE%D1%88%D1%83%D1%80%D0%B0%20-%20TMX7510.pdf) and pixel pitch calculation (800 x 18.5um = 14.8mm) say 23.7 x 14.8 mm.
- **Source B**: [Rev 7, May 2025 datasheet](https://www.phantomhighspeed.com/-/media/project/ameteksxa/visionresearch/documents/datasheets/web/ds_web-phantom-tmx-7510-6410-5010-rev6.pdf) reportedly says 23.7 x 15.4 mm.
- **Analysis**: The 14.8mm figure matches pixel calculation exactly. The 15.4mm figure matches the T4040/T2540 sensor height. For FOV calculations based on active pixels, 14.8mm (800 x 18.5um) should be used.

### Phantom Flex Original Frame Rates
- **Source A**: [New Atlas](https://newatlas.com/phantom-flex-camera-shoots-1080p-at-2800-frames-per-second/14842/) - 1,560 fps at 2560x1600 full res, 2,800 fps at 1080p, 6,100 fps at 720p.
- **Source B**: [AbelCine official announcement](https://www.abelcine.com/articles/news/product-news/vision-research-unveils-phantom-flex-next-gen-camera) - 1,459 fps at full res in Standard Mode, 2,571 fps at 1920x1080, 5,600 fps at 1280x720.
- **Analysis**: The higher numbers from New Atlas may reflect "HQ off" or later firmware. The AbelCine numbers come from the official Vision Research press release. Multiple speed modes exist on this camera.

### v2640 Sensor Size: 27.6x26.3 vs 35.8x22.4
- **Source A**: [Oplenyx](https://www.oplenyx.com/phantom-v2640-onyx/), [VFX Camera Database](https://vfxcamdb.com/vision-research-phantom-v2640-onyx/), [Y.M.Cinema T1340](https://ymcinema.com/2020/09/17/meet-the-high-speed-phantom-t1340-that-shoots-3270-fps-at-2k-resolution/) - 27.6 x 26.3 mm for both v2640 and T1340 (same sensor).
- **Source B**: Earlier search summary mentioned 35.8 x 22.4 mm -- this is the v2512 sensor size, NOT the v2640.
- **Resolution**: NOT a contradiction. The v2640 and v2512 have completely different sensors. v2640 = 13.5um pixels, 2048x1952, 27.6x26.3mm. v2512 = 28um pixels, 1280x800, 35.8x22.4mm.
