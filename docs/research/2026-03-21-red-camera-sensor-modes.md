- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Sensor**: Mysterium 12 MP CMOS, Active: 4520 x 2540 pixels, Sensor: 24.4 mm x 13.7 mm (Super 35)

The 3K frame rate cap of 60fps is inferred from the general "1-60fps for 4K/3K" specification found in one source; another source cites up to 120fps only at 2K.

## Contradictions

### RED ONE MX 4K Max Frame Rate
- **Source A**: Multiple sources say 30fps at 4K, 120fps at 2K (consistent with original RED ONE)
- **Source B**: One source (4K Shooters) claims the MX could do "up to 60fps at 4K" via the Mysterium-X upgrade
  - [4K Shooters](https://www.4kshooters.net/2023/04/10/how-good-is-a-16-year-old-red-one-in-2023/) - "Super 35mm Mysterium-X sensor could deliver REDCODE RAW video in 4K up to 60p"
  - [Hollywood Camera](https://hollywoodcamera.com/portfolio/red-one-mx/) - "up to 120 fps at 2K resolution and up to 30 fps at 4K"
  - **Resolution**: The discrepancy likely arises from confusing RED ONE MX with the EPIC-X (which uses the same Mysterium-X sensor but with a faster processing pipeline). The RED ONE body itself was limited to 30fps at 4K. I've used 30fps as the conservative figure.

### EPIC-X 5K Max Frame Rate
- **Source A**: Chater Camera lists 96fps at 5K FF
- **Source B**: Some sources claim 120fps at 5K
  - **Resolution**: The 96fps figure is the max for 5K FF (17:9); the 120fps figure likely refers to 5K WS (2.4:1). Both are correct for different modes.

### V-RAPTOR [X] vs V-RAPTOR 8K VV Frame Rates
- **Minor differences**: 7K is 140fps on [X] vs 137fps on original V-RAPTOR. This is due to the different sensor architecture (global shutter vs rolling shutter). Both come from official RED docs.
