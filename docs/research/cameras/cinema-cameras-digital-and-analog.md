## Finding D1: Panavision Genesis

- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Sensor size**: 23.62 mm x 13.28 mm (Super 35 equivalent)
- **Sensor type**: 12.4-megapixel single-chip CCD, RGB Bayer-pattern
- **Supported resolutions**: Native 5760 x 2160 RGB array, resampled to 1920 x 1080 (HD) output
- **Lens mount**: Panavision PV mount (same as 35mm film cameras)
- **Aspect ratio**: 16:9 (1.78:1) native sensor
- **Frame rates**: 1-50 fps
- **Color depth**: 10-bit log per channel
- **Native ISO**: 400
- **Introduced**: 2004
- **Discontinued**: 2012
- **Supporting sources**:
  - [Genesis (camera) - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genesis_(camera)) - "single 12.4 megapixel CCD chip, 5760x2160 RGB array, resampled to 1920x1080"

- **Introduced**: 2016
- **Supporting sources**:

## Finding F1: Standard Film Format Frame Sizes (Camera Aperture)

- **Confidence**: HIGH (cross-referenced across multiple sources)

All dimensions are **Width x Height in mm** and represent the **camera aperture** (exposed area on negative), not projector aperture.

| Format | Width (mm) | Height (mm) | Aspect Ratio | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------------|--------------|-------|
| Regular 8mm | 4.88 | 3.68 | 1.33:1 | Double-run 8mm |
| Super 8mm | 5.63 | 4.22 | 1.33:1 | Larger gate than Reg 8 |
| 16mm (Standard) | 10.26 | 7.49 | 1.37:1 | Single-perf or double-perf |
| Super 16mm | 12.52 | 7.42 | 1.69:1 | Extended into soundtrack area |
| 35mm Academy (4-perf) | 21.95 | 16.00 | 1.375:1 | SMPTE standard, post-1932 |
| 35mm Full Silent / Edison (4-perf) | 24.89 | 18.67 | 1.33:1 | No soundtrack area |
| 35mm 3-perf | 24.89 | 13.90 | 1.79:1 | ~16:9 native |
| 35mm 2-perf (Techniscope) | 22.05 | 9.47 | 2.33:1 | Half-height Academy |
| Super 35mm (4-perf) | 24.89 | 18.66 | 1.33:1 | Full silent aperture, no soundtrack |
| Super 35mm (3-perf) | 24.89 | 13.90 | 1.79:1 | Same width, 3-perf height |
| VistaVision (8-perf horizontal) | 37.72 | 24.92 | 1.51:1 | 35mm film run horizontally |
| 65mm 5-perf | 48.59 | 22.04 | 2.20:1 | Camera negative |
| 70mm 5-perf (print with soundtrack) | 48.59 | 22.04 | 2.20:1 | Same image area, wider stock for soundtrack |
| IMAX 15-perf 65mm | 70.41 | 52.63 | 1.34:1 | 65mm run horizontally, 15 perfs |

- **Supporting sources**:
  - [GCM Studio Film Specifications](http://www.gcmstudio.com/filmspecs/filmspecs.html) - primary source for camera aperture dimensions
  - [Wolfcrow - Film and Cinema Formats](https://wolfcrow.com/all-the-film-and-cinema-formats-you-should-be-aware-of/) - Academy 21.95x16.00, Super 35 24.89x18.66, VistaVision ~36x18.3
  - [In Depth Cine - 2-perf vs 3-perf vs 4-perf](https://www.indepthcine.com/videos/perf-35mm-film) - 4-perf: 18.7mm H, 3-perf: 13.9mm H, 2-perf: 9.35mm H, width 24.9mm
  - [Cinematography.net - IMAX specs](https://www.cinematography.net/edited-pages/IMAX15perf_70mmTechSpecs.htm) - "2.74 x 1.913 inches" (69.6 x 48.6mm)
  - [Matthew Wagenknecht - 35mm Formats](https://www.matthewwagenknecht.com/35mm-formats-and-aspect-ratios-explained/) - Academy 21.94x16mm, Silent/S35 24.89x18.66mm

- **Notes**: IMAX dimensions vary slightly across sources. GCM Studio lists 70.41 x 52.63 mm; cinematography.net discussion gives ~69.6 x 48.6 mm. The GCM Studio figure appears to be the camera aperture, while the smaller figure may be the projected aperture. The Super 35 dimension listed by GCM Studio as 24.92 x 10.6 mm appears erroneous (likely conflating S35 width with a cropped height); the standard S35 4-perf full gate is 24.89 x 18.66 mm per multiple other sources.

## ARRI Film Cameras

### Finding A1: ARRI 16SR Series (16SR, 16SR2, 16SR3)

- **Confidence**: HIGH
- **Film format**: 16mm / Super 16mm (switchable gate)
- **Frame size**: Standard 16mm: 10.26 x 7.49 mm; Super 16mm: 12.52 x 7.42 mm
- **Lens mount**:
  - 16SR (original): ARRI Bayonet mount
  - 16SR2 (early): ARRI Bayonet; (later): PL mount
  - 16SR3: PL mount
- **Frame rates**: 5-75 fps (standard); up to 150 fps (High Speed variant)
- **Shutter**: Mirror reflex
- **Power**: 12V DC (SR/SR2); 24V DC (SR3)
- **Film capacity**: 400 ft magazines (800 ft on SR3)
- **Introduced**: 1975 (original)
- **Supporting sources**:

### Finding A7: ARRI IIC / IIB / IIA (Arriflex 35 series)

- **Confidence**: HIGH

- **Type**: MOS camera (not self-blimped)
- **Introduced**: IIA (1953), IIB (1960), IIC (1964)
- **Production**: ~17,000 units total, 1946-1978
- **Supporting sources**:

## Contradictions

### IMAX Frame Dimensions
- **GCM Studio** says the IMAX 15-perf camera aperture is 70.41 x 52.63 mm
- **Cinematography.net discussion** gives "2.74 x 1.913 inches" = ~69.6 x 48.6 mm
- These likely represent camera aperture vs. projected aperture, or measurement methodology differences. The GCM Studio figure appears to be the larger camera aperture.
  - [GCM Studio](http://www.gcmstudio.com/filmspecs/filmspecs.html)
  - [Cinematography.net](https://www.cinematography.net/edited-pages/IMAX15perf_70mmTechSpecs.htm)

### Super 35 Height (GCM Studio anomaly)
- **GCM Studio** lists Super 35 as 24.92 x 10.6 mm, which would give a 2.35:1 ratio -- this appears to be a cropped/widescreen extraction, not the full Super 35 gate
- **All other sources** consistently give Super 35 4-perf as 24.89 x 18.66 mm (full gate, 1.33:1)
- The GCM Studio table likely shows a common projection extraction rather than the camera aperture
  - [GCM Studio](http://www.gcmstudio.com/filmspecs/filmspecs.html)
  - [Wolfcrow](https://wolfcrow.com/all-the-film-and-cinema-formats-you-should-be-aware-of/)

### VistaVision Dimensions
- **Wolfcrow** gives ~36 x 18.3 mm
- **GCM Studio** gives 37.59 x 24.89 mm
- **Matthew Wagenknecht** gives 24 x 36 mm (likely using photo convention H x W)
- The GCM Studio figure of 37.59 x 24.89 mm as camera aperture is most consistent with SMPTE standards
  - [GCM Studio](http://www.gcmstudio.com/filmspecs/filmspecs.html)
  - [Wolfcrow](https://wolfcrow.com/all-the-film-and-cinema-formats-you-should-be-aware-of/)

### Panavision DXL Sensor
- Some sources describe the original DXL sensor as "RED Dragon VV"
- Others say it was the "RED 8K sensor" without specifying Dragon vs Helium
- The DXL2 upgrade to RED Monstro 8K VV is unambiguous across all sources
